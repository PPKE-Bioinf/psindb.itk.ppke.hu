Selection of Postsynaptic Density (PSD) proteins

We used 4 resources to define the postsynaptic density proteome: SynaptomeDB (http://metamoodics.org/SynaptomeDB/index.php), G2C (https://www.genes2cognition.org/), SynGO (https://syngoportal.org/ )and Gene ontology annotations (http://geneontology.org/). For all proteins listed in any of these resources we collected interactions from various sources.

Comprehensive collection of PSD protein-protein interactions (PPIs)

We collected over 3000 experiments from the literature describing PPIs using MIMIx (https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/17687370/) standards. During the collection we aimed to look for papers that were not included in other similar resources. To have a more complete collection of PSD Interactome we also display interactions from IntAct (https://www.ebi.ac.uk/intact/) and BIOGRID (https://thebiogrid.org/) databases. In addition, we scanned the PDB database (https://www.rcsb.org/) for complexes that have interacting residues. Furthermore, computational evidences from the STRING (https://string-db.org/) database also displayed (above 0.8 cutoff score).

When defining PPIs it is a common and necessary step to check whether the proposed partners co-occure in the same cellular compartment, giving biological significance to the experiment. In this case, all proteins were collected from the same subcellular localization, thus further filters were not applied. However, we discarded interaction data that were only confirmed using co-localization or proximity.
